11 hours later...
... I arrived at Pichanaki, a rather big city for the Selva, which was, in my regards, characterised by the large main road. It was the first time I had seen a road, divided into multiple lanes in such a fashion. Anyways, at 7 o'clock in the morning, the traffic was buzzing with moto-taxis, pick-ups and different small and big trucks pobably on the way to work. Apparently, rush hour is a global phenomenon. Tired and a bit lost, Erik and I waited for Aurelia, with whom I was chatting with, to take care of our arrival. After I had confidently pointed at a few wrong persons, saying they were Aurelia solely based on a blurry profile picture, a pick-up arrived with her and a few other people sitting inside.
